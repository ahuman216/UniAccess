About UniAccess

Built on empathy. Grounded in universal design. Free for everyone.

Ethical Design Statement

UniAccess was built with one belief: technology should work for every person, regardless of their sensory ability, economic status, or technical literacy.

We follow the principles of Universal Design — the idea that products should be usable by all people, to the greatest extent possible, without the need for adaptation or specialised design. Accessibility is not a feature. It is the foundation.

This application collects zero personal data, displays zero advertisements, and requires zero account creation. All preferences are stored locally on your device and never transmitted anywhere.

Real-World Impact

Education

Students who are deaf or hard-of-hearing often rely on sign-language interpreters — a resource many schools cannot afford. LiveSpeak provides free, real-time captioning so every student can follow a lecture.

Students with low vision or dyslexia can use ReadAssist to transform handout text into a format that reduces reading fatigue and increases comprehension.

Public Spaces & Safety

Traditional fire alarms and emergency announcements depend entirely on hearing. SafeAlert delivers alerts through screen flash, vibration, and on-screen text — ensuring no one is left uninformed in an emergency.

VisionSense helps users with vision impairments navigate unfamiliar spaces by identifying objects and printed signs through the camera.

WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

Every page in UniAccess is designed to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.1, Level AA .

Keyboard Navigation

Every interactive element is reachable and operable via keyboard alone.

Screen Reader Support

ARIA landmarks, labels, live regions, and roles throughout every page.

Colour Contrast ≥ 4.5:1

All text and background colour pairs exceed the WCAG minimum contrast ratio.

Focus Indicators

Visible focus rings on all interactive elements — never suppressed for keyboard users.

No Seizure Risk

Flashing animations are limited to ≤ 3 per second (WCAG 2.3.1) and respect prefers-reduced-motion.

Semantic Heading Structure

Proper h1 → h4 hierarchy on every page for screen-reader navigation.

Universal Design Principles Applied

  1. Equitable Use — All tools are free and work across device types.
  2. Flexibility in Use — Users can pick audio, visual, or haptic outputs independently.
  3. Simple and Intuitive — Plain-language labels, large buttons, no jargon.
  4. Perceptible Information — Information conveyed via text, audio, and visual cues — never relying on one sense alone.
  5. Tolerance for Error — Destructive actions are undoable; errors are explained clearly.
  6. Low Physical Effort — Touch targets meet the 44px minimum; keyboard shortcuts available.
  7. Size and Space for Approach — Responsive design works from 320px mobile widths upward.
